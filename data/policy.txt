ACME CORPORATION — TRAVEL & EXPENSE POLICY
Version 4.2 | Effective: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: CFO & Legal

==============================================================
SECTION 1: SCOPE AND PURPOSE
==============================================================
This policy applies to all full-time employees, contractors, and consultants who incur business expenses on behalf of Acme Corporation. The purpose is to ensure that company funds are spent responsibly, that employees are reimbursed fairly and promptly, and that all expenditures comply with applicable tax laws and internal controls.

All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of the transaction date. Late submissions will not be reimbursed without written VP approval. Receipts are mandatory for all expenses exceeding $25 USD (or local equivalent).

==============================================================
SECTION 2: MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT
==============================================================
DOMESTIC MEAL LIMITS (Per Person):
- Breakfast: Maximum $20 USD
- Lunch: Maximum $35 USD
- Dinner: Maximum $75 USD
- Daily meal cap (all meals combined): Maximum $100 USD

REGIONAL MEAL LIMITS — HIGH COST CITIES (Per Person):
The following cities are classified as Tier 1 High-Cost and carry elevated limits:
New York City, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Boston, Chicago, Seattle, Washington D.C., London, Tokyo, Zurich, Singapore, Sydney
- Breakfast: Maximum $30 USD
- Lunch: Maximum $50 USD
- Dinner: Maximum $100 USD
- Daily meal cap: Maximum $150 USD

MEAL RULES:
- Alcohol is strictly NOT reimbursable unless explicitly pre-approved in writing by a VP or above.
- All meal receipts must include the names of all attendees and the stated business purpose.
- Meals for more than 10 people require Director-level pre-approval.
- Team meals are reimbursable up to $75 per person with manager approval.
- Client entertainment meals are reimbursable up to $100 per person with manager approval.
- Personal meals during non-travel days are not reimbursable.
- Room service is reimbursable only during approved travel, subject to the dinner limit.

PROHIBITED MEAL EXPENSES:
- Alcohol, minibar charges, and personal snacks are not reimbursable.
- Meals claimed on weekends without a documented client or business event are flagged for review.
- Meals for family members or non-employees are not reimbursable.

==============================================================
SECTION 3: DOMESTIC AND INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL
==============================================================
AIR TRAVEL:
- All domestic flights must be booked in Economy class.
- Business class is permitted only for flights exceeding 6 hours of total flight time.
- First class is never reimbursable under any circumstances.
- Flights must be booked at least 14 days in advance to qualify for reimbursement. Last-minute bookings require VP approval.
- Employees must use the company's preferred travel booking portal (Concur).

HOTEL AND LODGING:
- Standard cities: Maximum $200 USD per night.
- Tier 1 High-Cost cities (see Section 2): Maximum $350 USD per night.
- International travel: Maximum $400 USD per night.
- Hotel stays must be within reasonable proximity to the business destination.
- Resort fees, spa charges, and in-room movies are not reimbursable.
- Laundry is reimbursable for trips exceeding 5 consecutive nights.

GROUND TRANSPORTATION:
- Taxis, rideshares (Uber, Lyft), and public transit are fully reimbursable with receipts.
- Car rentals are reimbursable for trips where a rental is more cost-effective than rideshares. Economy class only.
- Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate ($0.67/mile as of 2025).
- Parking fees at airports or client sites are reimbursable.
- Tolls are reimbursable.
- Traffic fines and parking violations are NOT reimbursable.

INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL:
- Requires VP approval at least 7 days before travel.
- Travel insurance is mandatory and reimbursable.
- Currency exchange fees are reimbursable.
- Visa and passport fees for business travel are reimbursable.

==============================================================
SECTION 4: ACCOMMODATION AND LODGING
==============================================================
- Hotel bookings must be made through the company's approved booking portal.
- Airbnb and similar short-term rentals are permitted only when hotel options are unavailable or exceed the nightly limit, with manager approval.
- Extended stays (more than 14 nights) require Director approval and may qualify for a negotiated corporate rate.
- Incidental charges (phone calls, gym access, minibar) are not reimbursable.

==============================================================
SECTION 5: OFFICE SUPPLIES AND EQUIPMENT
==============================================================
- Office supplies up to $50 per month do not require pre-approval.
- Office supplies between $50 and $200 require manager approval.
- Equipment purchases (laptops, monitors, peripherals) over $200 require Director pre-approval before purchase.
- Software subscriptions must be approved by the IT department prior to purchase, regardless of cost.
- Standing desks and ergonomic equipment up to $500 are reimbursable once every 3 years with manager approval.
- Personal printers and home office furniture are not reimbursable.

==============================================================
SECTION 6: CONFERENCES, TRAINING, AND PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
==============================================================
- Conference registration fees are reimbursable with manager approval.
- Training courses and professional certifications are reimbursable up to $1,500 per employee per calendar year.
- Books and learning materials related to job function are reimbursable up to $200 per year.
- Professional membership dues are reimbursable up to $300 per year with manager approval.
- Conferences requiring travel are subject to all travel policy rules in Section 3.

==============================================================
SECTION 7: CLIENT GIFTS AND ENTERTAINMENT
==============================================================
- Client gifts are reimbursable up to $50 per client per year.
- Gifts to employees exceeding $25 in value require HR approval.
- Gift cards are not reimbursable as client gifts.
- Entertainment (sporting events, concerts, theatre) is reimbursable up to $150 per person per event with VP approval.
- Entertainment must have a documented business purpose and client attendee list.

==============================================================
SECTION 8: TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND TECHNOLOGY
==============================================================
- Business mobile phone plans are reimbursable up to $80 per month.
- International roaming charges during approved travel are reimbursable.
- Home internet for remote employees is reimbursable up to $50 per month.
- Personal streaming services, gaming subscriptions, and entertainment apps are not reimbursable.

==============================================================
SECTION 9: HEALTH, WELLNESS, AND PERSONAL EXPENSES
==============================================================
- Gym memberships and personal fitness equipment are NOT reimbursable.
- Medical expenses are not reimbursable through this policy (use health insurance).
- Personal grooming (haircuts, spa, salon) is not reimbursable.
- Childcare and pet care during travel are not reimbursable.
- Clothing and personal items are not reimbursable, except for safety equipment required on-site.

==============================================================
SECTION 10: PROHIBITED EXPENSES (ABSOLUTE)
==============================================================
The following expenses will NEVER be reimbursed under any circumstances:
- Alcohol (unless VP-approved in writing for client entertainment)
- Personal vacations or leisure travel
- Fines, penalties, and legal fees
- Gambling or lottery tickets
- Political or charitable donations
- Luxury goods (watches, jewelry, designer items)
- First-class airfare
- Traffic violations and parking fines
- Personal entertainment unrelated to business
- Expenses for family members or non-employees
- Duplicate submissions (will result in disciplinary action)

==============================================================
SECTION 11: SUBMISSION AND APPROVAL WORKFLOW
==============================================================
- All expenses must be submitted via the company expense portal within 30 days.
- Receipts are required for all expenses over $25.
- Expenses between $500–$1,000 require manager approval.
- Expenses over $1,000 require Director approval.
- Expenses over $5,000 require VP approval.
- Finance team will process approved claims within 5–7 business days.
- Disputed claims will be reviewed within 10 business days.
- Employees may appeal a rejected claim once by submitting additional documentation.

==============================================================
SECTION 12: COMPLIANCE AND CONSEQUENCES
==============================================================
- Submitting false or inflated expense claims constitutes fraud and may result in immediate termination.
- Employees found to have submitted duplicate claims will face disciplinary action.
- Managers who approve non-compliant claims share responsibility for the violation.
- All expense data is subject to periodic internal audit.
- Expenses that appear excessive, unusual, or inconsistent with business purpose will be escalated to the Finance Director.
